LLM & AI-Agent Ready (AEO)

Artificial Intelligence search bots and RAG engines crawl semantic schemas. By presenting a fully normalized, pre-linked relational design, AI models can write perfect SQL queries to answer complex, multi-hop sports questions (e.g., player card frequency analysis in high-altitude stadiums) without hallucination.

High-Authority SEO Hook

Search engines reward data consistency and rich relational structures. This SQLite-ready database model links squads, matches, venues, and live events, allowing indexers to crawl clean, non-duplicate schemas and boost authority rankings on World Cup dataset searches.

Zero Duplication Integrity

Flat CSV tables often result in out-of-sync player names, duplicate schedules, or orphaned records. Our fully normalized SQLite database ensures 100% mathematical integrity across all 11 tables, providing a robust, plug-and-play local structure for analytics portfolios.

Relational Schema & Table Definitions

Table Name Primary Key Key Foreign Keys Description
teams team_id - Complete list of all 48 participating countries, FIFA codes, ELO ratings, and managers.
venues venue_id - Information on the 16 host stadiums, including city, capacity, coordinates, and elevations.
matches match_id stage_id, venue_id, home_team_id, away_team_id, referee_id Match schedules, scorelines, expected goals (xG), and official referees.
squads_and_players player_id team_id Comprehensive registry of all 1,248 players, club teams, market values, and international caps.
match_events event_id match_id, team_id, player_id Minute-by-minute timeline of goals, assists, yellow/red cards, and VAR events.
match_team_stats match_id, team_id match_id, team_id Per-team metrics: possession %, total shots, corners, saves, and fouls.
match_lineups lineup_id match_id, player_id, team_id Tactical lineups detailing starting XIs, substitutes, and exact minutes played.
player_stats player_id player_id, team_id Cumulative tournament player statistics (goals, assists, clean sheets, saves, etc.).

World Cup 2026 Sports Analytics & SQL Project Ideas

Target search queries from data scientists, analysts, and students looking for portfolio project inspiration.

🏆 1. Build a SQL Data Analytics Portfolio

Practice relational joins, subqueries, and window functions. Here are some SQL query ideas you can build with this dataset:

  • Card Frequency Analysis: Query which referees award the most yellow and red cards per match, and join with referee overall averages to find strictness percentiles.
  • Altitude & Possession Correlation: Join the venues table with match stats to analyze whether matches played in high-altitude stadiums (like Estadio Azteca at 2,200m or Estadio BBVA) exhibit lower possession rates or fewer second-half shots.
  • Market Value vs. Performance: Join player market values from transfer market estimates with tournament statistics to find the most cost-effective goalscorers and defenders.

📊 2. Predict Match Outcomes with Machine Learning

Build prediction models using our pre-compiled feature metrics in match_prediction_features.csv.

  • Knockout Stage Classifier: Train classifiers (e.g., XGBoost, Random Forest) using FIFA rankings, ELO ratings, historical head-to-heads, and squad market values to predict progress.
  • Expected Goals (xG) Analysis: Correlate live expected goals (xG) differences with actual scorelines to find teams that outperformed their analytical baseline projections.
  • Travel & Rest Day Effects: Analyze how different travel distances between venues and rest intervals affect ELO performance decay curves.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Direct factual answers optimizing for AI search summaries and voice crawls.

How often is the World Cup 2026 dataset updated?

The dataset is updated daily, immediately following the conclusion of each World Cup match. Updates include final scorelines, detailed team stats, expected goals (xG), minute-by-minute events, and player statistics.

Is there any synthetic or simulated data?

No. We enforce a strict Data Integrity Policy: no synthetic or estimated match statistics are allowed. If verified data for a specific metric is temporarily unavailable, it remains empty (NULL) rather than filled with simulated stats.

Can I use this dataset for commercial purposes?

Yes. The dataset is published under the Creative Commons Zero v1.0 Universal (CC0 1.0) license. It is in the public domain, meaning you can copy, modify, distribute, and perform the work, even for commercial purposes, without asking permission.

Is this dataset ready for SQL portolio projects?

Yes. The database has been normalized into 11 tables with proper primary and foreign keys. This makes it perfect for SQL practice, Power BI / Tableau dashboards, or data engineering pipelines.
